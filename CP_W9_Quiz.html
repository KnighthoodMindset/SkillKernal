Cybersecurity - Overall Quiz

What is the earliest notion of privacy in history?





Correct Answer: Privacy as freedom, emphasizing the right to be let alone

True or False: Privacy is an absolute right in the context of law.



Correct Answer: False

The acronym FIPP stands for:





Correct Answer: Fair Information Practice Principles

The __________ view argues that privacy is not an independent concept but can be reduced to other rights like property or liberty.





Correct Answer: Reductionist

What is the core principle of informational privacy?





Correct Answer: The ability of individuals to decide what personal information they share and with whom

The FIPPs were first introduced in a report by:





Correct Answer: The US Secretary's Advisory Committee on Automated Personal Data Systems

According to India's DPDP Act, what/who is the 'data principle'?





Correct Answer: The individual whose data is being processed/used

Which of the following aspects of data privacy is NOT explicitly addressed in the FIPP?





Correct Answer: The right to be forgotten

Under India's DPDP Act, which of the following correctly describes the role of a Data Processor?





Correct Answer: The entity that processes personal data on behalf of the Data Fiduciary

Which of the following are important reasons why information privacy is significant for organizations? (Select all that apply. More than one answer may be possible)






Correct Answers: Protect sensitive customer and employee data from unauthorized access; Comply with legal and regulatory requirements related to data protection; To maintain customer trust and protect the organization's reputation; Avoid financial penalties and legal consequences due to data breaches
