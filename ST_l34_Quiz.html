Software testing - lecture 34 - Quiz

What is the primary focus of syntax-based testing?





Syntax-based testing focuses on exploiting syntactical structures of software to generate valid and invalid test cases.

Which levels of programming language syntax are primarily considered in syntax-based testing?





Lexical and phrase levels are focused on because they directly describe the structure for generating test cases.

What is the main role of regular expressions in syntax-based testing?





Regular expressions define how characters combine to form valid tokens at the lexical level.

Which theorem establishes the relationship between regular expressions and finite automata?





Kleene’s theorem states that every regular expression corresponds to a finite automaton and vice versa.

What defines a context-free grammar?





Context-free grammars consist of terminals, non-terminals, a start symbol, and production rules replacing a single non-terminal.

What is the purpose of terminal symbol coverage in syntax-based testing?





Terminal symbol coverage ensures each terminal symbol is included in at least one generated test case.

Which coverage criterion ensures that every production rule is used at least once?





Production coverage ensures that each production rule in the grammar is used at least once.

Why is derivation coverage often infeasible in syntax-based testing?





Derivation coverage is infeasible because recursive grammars can produce an infinite number of strings.

At the lexical level, what is a token?





A token is a valid lexical unit formed from characters, such as identifiers, numbers, or keywords.

What is the main benefit of syntax-based testing for software components?





Syntax-based testing improves robustness by validating correct handling of valid and invalid inputs.
