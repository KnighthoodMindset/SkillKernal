Software testing - Week 2 - lecture 8 - Quiz

1. What is the main difference between Depth First Search (DFS) and Breadth First Search (BFS)?





DFS dives deep into the graph before backtracking, while BFS explores all neighbors level by level.

2. What do the colors white, gray, and black represent in DFS?





In DFS, white means unvisited, gray means discovered but not fully explored, and black means completely explored.

3. What are the two timestamps maintained in DFS for each vertex?





DFS records discovery time when a vertex is first encountered and finish time after all its adjacent vertices are explored.

4. What is the time complexity of Depth First Search (DFS)?





DFS runs in linear time proportional to the sum of vertices and edges, O(V + E).

5. What is a Depth-First Forest?





When DFS runs on a disconnected graph, each call creates a tree; all trees together form a depth-first forest.

6. Which of the following is NOT a type of edge identified during DFS traversal?





DFS classifies edges as tree, back, forward, or cross edges. Sequential edge is not among them.

7. What is a Strongly Connected Component (SCC)?





An SCC is a maximal subgraph in which every vertex is reachable from every other vertex.

8. What is the purpose of the transpose graph in Kosaraju’s algorithm?





The transpose graph reverses all edge directions and helps reveal SCCs during the second DFS pass.

9. In Kosaraju’s algorithm, in what order are vertices processed during the second DFS?





Vertices are processed in decreasing order of their finish times from the first DFS to correctly identify SCCs.

10. What type of graph is formed when each Strongly Connected Component is represented as a single node?





When SCCs are treated as single nodes, the resulting component graph is always a Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG).
