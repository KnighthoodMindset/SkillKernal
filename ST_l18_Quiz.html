Software testing - lecture 18 - Quiz

What do design specifications describe in software development?





Design specifications describe module structure, interactions, and constraints to ensure correct design.

What are sequencing constraints in design specifications?





Sequencing constraints dictate the correct order of method or operation invocation.

Why are sequencing constraints often not included in formal requirement documents?





Sequencing constraints are defined by designers to ensure correct module interactions and design integrity.

What is a "must" violation in sequencing constraints?





A "must" violation indicates a definite fault in the sequencing constraints.

Which technique analyzes control flow graphs without executing the program to check sequencing constraints?





Static testing analyzes the control flow graph to identify potential violations without program execution.

How does dynamic testing verify sequencing constraints?





Dynamic testing executes the program to verify if paths violating sequencing constraints are actual faults.

What type of sequencing constraints require tracking historical behavior or number of operations?





Stateful sequencing constraints track historical behavior or the number of operations.

Which models are typically used to express and verify stateful sequencing constraints?





Finite state machines are used to express and verify stateful sequencing constraints.

What is the purpose of constructing test paths that deliberately attempt violation of sequencing constraints?





Deliberately violating sequencing constraints helps detect faults in design or implementation.

Why are sequencing constraints crucial in integration testing?





Sequencing constraints ensure modules interact correctly, maintain operation order, and uphold design integrity.
